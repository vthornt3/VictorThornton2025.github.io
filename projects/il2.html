Secondary-Supplier IL-2 Comparability for CAR-T Expansion

Qualified alternate IL-2 suppliers and dose levels against a reference condition for T-cell expansion, using standardized culture, analytics, and statistical comparability criteria.

Overview

To de-risk supply chain interruptions, I led a study to compare secondary IL-2 sources and concentrations against a reference process for CAR-T expansion. The work covered experimental design, execution on multiple donors, and analysis across population doubling level (PDL), viable cell density (VCD), viability, and molecular comparability.

My Role

  • Designed screening & follow-up matrices; defined acceptance criteria and endpoints.
  • Ran cultures and daily analytics; maintained data integrity & traceability.
  • Performed ANOVA/DOE in JMP/Minitab; generated comparability reports.
  • Authored protocol, data sheets, and a tech-transfer summary.

Problem → Approach → Outcome

Problem: Ensure an alternative IL-2 supplier (and dose window) can replace the primary without impacting cell growth kinetics or quality attributes.

Approach: Screening across brand × dose, followed by confirmation runs on additional donor(s). Endpoints included PDL, VCD, % viability, and ELISA-based molecular comparability. Statistical tests assessed equivalence against the reference condition.

Outcome: Identified dose/supplier conditions comparable to the reference, enabling a qualified backup path. (Insert your specific thresholds and summary stats below.)

Study Design & Methods (high level)

  • Culture system: G-Rex 6-well; typical well load ~20 M cells in 6.6 mL; TexMACS + 3% AB serum.
  • Activation/Transduction: TransAct on day 0; lentiviral transduction day 1 (MOI via titer calc); media refreshes per schedule.
  • Dosing: Reference IL-2 condition vs. alternate suppliers at multiple concentrations (screen → confirm).
  • Measurements: Daily/alt-day counts for viability, VCD, cell diameter; PDL computed over 8 days.
  • Analytics: ELISA for molecular comparability; JMP/Minitab for ANOVA & intervals.

Results (placeholders — swap in your numbers)

  • Viability: ≥ 96% across groups; end-of-run values within ±0.5% of each other.
  • PDL: Higher at increased IL-2 (up to ~3.75) vs. ~3.0 at lower concentrations.
  • VCD: Comparable to reference within 20% window by day 8.
  • ELISA: Target markers within acceptance limits (CV ≤ 15%).

Acceptance & Reporting

  • Pre-defined comparability windows for PDL/VCD/viability; equivalence tests where appropriate.
  • Traceability from study plan → raw data → analysis → decision memo.
  • Handoff packet: protocol, filled data sheets, JMP project file, and a one-page executive summary.
