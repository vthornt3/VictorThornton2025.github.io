Getting to Know Me

I’m here to make care easier on families like mine—because the stakes are personal.

Before my senior year, things changed for my family. Within 3 months of one another, my dad broke his hip due to military service connected Parkinson's and Dementia related psychosis, and my mom found a brain tumor. We have been figuring out our changed home routines and navigating an ocean of systems - including the VA process, which can be a daunting and frustrating experience. From my parents, I learned that serving others and making a positive impact was a valuable compass to have.

This compass has guided me in my academic and professional pursuits. One moment that still resonates with me: two weeks of waiting for biopsy results after my mom's surgery. The delay and uncertainty instigated a desire to work on the science and tools that improve patient outcomes and streamline healthcare processes. It is a part of why I chose to take a path in biotech and medical devices; it is my hope that patients and families like mine may benefit from the advancements I create.

When I am not focused on academics or working in industry, I am outside. I have visited 14 national parks in the last three years! Within the next ten years I plan to see all 63 of them. I am also a proud Southeast Texas from Beaumont, and I love to cook. Sharing food and communion with others is an act of love that stems from my Hispanic culture and I am always open to sharing the recipes I know with the team. I value kindness under pressure, clarity, and follow-through. With these I believe real change can be made.
